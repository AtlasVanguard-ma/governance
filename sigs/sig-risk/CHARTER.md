# SIG-RISK Charter

## 1. Purpose

SIG-RISK is responsible for developing structured methods to understand, measure, and monitor systemic risk within Morocco’s evolving financial markets.

This includes studying how instability emerges, propagates, and amplifies across assets, sectors, and market regimes.

The SIG focuses on building reproducible risk observability rather than trading or forecasting systems.

---

## 2. Scope

SIG-RISK covers:

- Systemic risk measurement frameworks
- Market stress detection and monitoring
- Volatility regime identification
- Correlation breakdown analysis
- Contagion dynamics across sectors and indices
- Exposure concentration and imbalance detection
- Macro-financial risk indicators

---

## 3. Out of Scope

SIG-RISK does NOT cover:

- Trading strategies or execution systems
- Investment decision-making or advisory output
- Portfolio optimization for capital allocation
- Speculative forecasting without structural analysis
- Brokerage or financial product design

---

## 4. Objectives

The SIG aims to:

- Define measurable systemic risk indicators for Moroccan markets
- Identify early signals of financial stress and instability
- Study structural behavior during volatility regimes
- Improve understanding of cross-market contagion effects
- Contribute to a shared risk observability layer within AtlasVanguard

---

## 5. Key Outputs

SIG-RISK produces:

- Risk metrics definitions and specifications
- Stress indicator frameworks
- Correlation instability analysis
- Regime classification methodologies
- Documentation of systemic risk models
- Inputs to governance RFCs affecting risk modeling

---

## 6. Operating Principles

SIG-RISK operates under the following principles:

- **Reproducibility**: All risk measures must be computable from defined data inputs
- **Transparency**: Methods must be documented and explainable
- **Separation of concerns**: Risk analysis is distinct from decision-making systems
- **Market realism**: Models must reflect actual market structure, not abstract assumptions
- **Cross-SIG alignment**: Risk definitions must be consistent with Quant and Market Structure SIGs

---

## 7. Interfaces with Other SIGs

SIG-RISK collaborates with:

- **SIG-QUANT** → volatility, beta, correlation models used in risk systems
- **SIG-MARKET-STRUCTURE** → structural interpretation of risk signals
- **SIG-DATA-INFRASTRUCTURE** → data quality and consistency for risk computation
- **SIG-OBSERVABILITY-COMMUNICATION** → visualization of stress and risk indicators

---

## 8. Governance

Changes to risk definitions or systemic metrics require:

- RFC submission for methodological changes
- Review by SIG-RISK members
- Cross-SIG validation when impacting shared metrics
- Approval by Steering Committee for system-wide adoption

---

## 9. Membership Expectations

Members of SIG-RISK are expected to:

- Understand basic financial market behavior (equities, indices, derivatives context)
- Contribute to structured thinking about systemic behavior
- Participate in discussions with clarity and rigor
- Avoid ambiguous or non-reproducible risk definitions

---

## 10. Deliverables Standard

All SIG-RISK outputs should:

- Be mathematically or logically defined
- Be implementable or testable
- Be versioned when updated
- Be documented in GitHub (not only discussed in Slack)

---

## 11. Guiding Principle

SIG-RISK exists to answer:

> “Where and how does financial instability emerge in the system, and how can it be observed before it propagates?”
