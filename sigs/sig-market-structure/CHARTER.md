# SIG Charter: Market Structure (SIG-MARKET-STRUCTURE)

## 1. Purpose

SIG-MARKET-STRUCTURE defines and governs the interpretation of how Moroccan financial markets are organized and how they behave as a system.

It focuses on understanding market mechanics, interactions between instruments, and structural evolution over time.

---

## 2. Mission

The mission of SIG-MARKET-STRUCTURE is to provide a consistent, research-driven framework for analyzing:

- how price discovery occurs in Moroccan markets
- how different instruments interact (spot, indices, futures)
- how liquidity and participation shape market behavior
- how structural changes affect financial stability and correlations

---

## 3. Scope

### In Scope

SIG-MARKET-STRUCTURE governs:

- relationships between indices, sectors, and instruments
- market segmentation (equities, indices, derivatives)
- price discovery dynamics between related instruments
- liquidity structure and observable market behavior
- interaction between benchmark indices and constituent assets
- structural interpretation of market evolution over time

---

### Out of Scope

SIG-MARKET-STRUCTURE does NOT govern:

- mathematical definitions of returns or volatility (SIG-QUANT)
- risk modeling (SIG-RISK)
- data ingestion or storage mechanisms (SIG-DATA-INFRASTRUCTURE)
- visualization or reporting layers (SIG-OBSERVABILITY-COMMUNICATION)
- formal model definitions (MODEL-GOVERNANCE)

---

## 4. Authority

SIG-MARKET-STRUCTURE has authority to:

- define structural interpretations of market relationships
- propose and review RFCs related to market behavior
- define conceptual frameworks for market interaction analysis
- validate assumptions about market linkage and structure

SIG-MARKET-STRUCTURE does NOT have authority to:

- define mathematical formulas or statistical models
- override quantitative definitions established by SIG-QUANT
- modify model definitions without MODEL-GOVERNANCE approval
- enforce data-level changes

---

## 5. Interfaces

SIG-MARKET-STRUCTURE interacts with:

- SIG-QUANT -> ensures structural concepts align with correct quantitative definitions
- SIG-RISK -> provides structural context for stress and instability analysis
- SIG-DATA-INFRASTRUCTURE -> ensures structural interpretation matches available data
- SIG-OBSERVABILITY-COMMUNICATION -> translates structural insights into reports and dashboards
- MODEL-GOVERNANCE -> formalizes structural models when needed

---

## 6. Responsibilities

SIG-MARKET-STRUCTURE is responsible for:

- defining market structure research questions
- maintaining clarity of instrument relationships (spot, index, futures)
- identifying structural shifts in market behavior
- ensuring consistency in interpretation of market linkages
- reviewing RFCs that affect market structure assumptions

---

## 7. Decision Rights

SIG-MARKET-STRUCTURE may:

- approve or reject structural interpretation proposals within its scope
- request RFCs for changes in market structure definitions
- escalate cross-domain issues to the Steering Committee

SIG-MARKET-STRUCTURE may NOT:

- define or modify quantitative models
- override SIG-QUANT definitions
- change system-wide benchmarks or data models independently

---

## 8. Governance Model

All structural changes that affect interpretation of markets must go through the RFC process.

This includes:

- redefining relationships between spot and futures markets
- introducing new structural segmentation of markets
- changing interpretation of sector or index relationships
- formalizing new structural hypotheses

---

## 9. Design Principle

SIG-MARKET-STRUCTURE operates under the principle:

> “Market behavior must be interpreted consistently as a system, not as isolated instruments.”

---

## 10. Success Criteria

SIG-MARKET-STRUCTURE is successful if:

- market relationships are clearly defined and consistent
- structural interpretations do not conflict across SIGs
- interactions between instruments are well understood
- changes in market structure are traceable and documented
