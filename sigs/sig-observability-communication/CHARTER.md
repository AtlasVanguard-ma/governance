# SIG-Observability-Communication Charter

## 1. Purpose

SIG-Observability-Communication is responsible for translating quantitative and structural financial outputs into clear, interpretable, and usable representations.

It defines how market behavior, risk signals, and analytical results are communicated, visualized, and made observable across AtlasVanguard.

The SIG acts as the interface layer between raw analytical systems and human understanding.

---

## 2. Scope

SIG-Observability-Communication covers:

- Design of financial dashboards and visual outputs
- Structuring of analytical reporting formats
- Visualization of risk, volatility, and correlation signals
- Communication of systemic behavior and regime changes
- Presentation standards for quantitative outputs
- Human-readable interpretation layers of model outputs
- Consistent reporting of cross-SIG results

---

## 3. Out of Scope

SIG-Observability-Communication does NOT cover:

- Definition of financial models or metrics
- Construction of quantitative engines
- Raw data ingestion or transformation logic
- Trading or investment-related interpretation
- Decision-making on risk or market structure

---

## 4. Objectives

The SIG aims to:

- Make complex financial and systemic outputs interpretable
- Ensure consistency in how analytics are presented across AtlasVanguard
- Reduce ambiguity in communication of quantitative results
- Improve accessibility of market structure insights for non-technical stakeholders
- Provide a standardized observability layer across SIG outputs

---

## 5. Key Outputs

SIG-Observability-Communication produces:

- Dashboard and visualization standards
- Reporting templates for SIG outputs
- Risk and stress visualization frameworks
- Correlation and beta interpretability layers
- Communication guidelines for analytical results
- Structured summaries of quantitative findings

---

## 6. Operating Principles

SIG-Observability-Communication operates under:

- **Clarity over complexity**: outputs must be understandable
- **Faithfulness**: visualizations must accurately reflect underlying data
- **Consistency**: same metrics must be represented uniformly across SIGs
- **Separation of concerns**: visualization does not alter model logic
- **Reproducibility**: all displayed outputs must trace back to computable sources

---

## 7. Interfaces with Other SIGs

SIG-Observability-Communication collaborates with:

- **SIG-QUANT** → representation of returns, volatility, beta, and models
- **SIG-RISK** → visualization of stress, instability, and systemic signals
- **SIG-MARKET-STRUCTURE** → communication of structural market behavior
- **SIG-DATA-INFRASTRUCTURE** → structured data feeding observability systems

---

## 8. Governance

All communication standards and observability conventions must:

- Be documented in GitHub
- Be reviewed through RFCs when changing system-wide formats
- Maintain compatibility with existing SIG outputs
- Avoid introducing interpretation bias into quantitative results

---

## 9. Membership Expectations

Members of SIG-Observability-Communication are expected to:

- Understand how quantitative outputs are generated (at a high level)
- Be able to translate technical results into structured narratives
- Maintain consistency across visual and textual representations
- Collaborate with technical SIGs to ensure accurate communication

---

## 10. Deliverables Standard

All outputs must:

- Be grounded in validated data or models
- Be traceable to a defined computation source
- Avoid speculative interpretation
- Be version-controlled when formats evolve

---

## 11. Guiding Principle

SIG-Observability-Communication exists to answer:

> “How do we make complex financial and systemic signals understandable without losing their technical integrity?”
